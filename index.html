Modeling and Control of Wave Patterns and Nonlinear System Dynamics

Sayak Bhattacharya is a fifth year PhD student in the lab of Dr. Pablo A. Iglesias at Johns Hopkins, working on modeling cortical wave patterns and dynamics of feedback signaling pathways — in applications of cell migration and thalamocortical networks.

Research Projects

Keywords: Wave propagation, Feedback pathways, Excitable dynamics, Cortical networks

Wave Patterns

Using wave theory to control and alter wave characteristics to create diverse patterns such as traveling, rotating and standing waves.
Relevant Publications: Link 1, Link 2

Cell Migration

Designing and incorporating controllers to create efficiently migrating cells that have increased speed and directional sensing.
Relevant Publications: Link 1, Link 2

Signaling Pathways

Building networks of signaling species that control cellular dynamics in order to model and predict protrusive behavior.
Relevant Publications: Link 1, Link 2

Thalamocortical Networks

Modeling the thalamocortical system as a two-dimensional three-layered structure that interacts in a closed-loop fashion to study traveling waves.
